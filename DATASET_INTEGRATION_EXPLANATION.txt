================================================================================
3.4 DATA COLLECTION AND INTEGRATION
================================================================================

The DiaTracker system integrates THREE separate datasets to train the machine learning models and provide nutritional information to users. This section explains each dataset, how they are combined, and the rationale for the integration approach.


3.4.1 Overview of Three Datasets
---------------------------------

DATASET 1: NHANES 2021-2023 (Health and Dietary Data)
Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
Size: 2,660 participants
Purpose: Training data for both Random Forest models
Contains: Demographics, body measurements, glucose levels, dietary intake

DATASET 2: Foster-Powell 2002 (Glycemic Index Values)
Source: Peer-reviewed journal article (PDF extraction)
Size: 200 foods
Purpose: Glycemic Index and Glycemic Load values for foods
Contains: Food names, GI values, GL values, serving sizes

DATASET 3: USDA FoodData Central (Nutritional Composition)
Source: U.S. Department of Agriculture
Size: 343,877 foods
Purpose: Complete nutritional information for food database
Contains: Calories, carbohydrates, protein, fat, fiber, sodium


3.4.2 Dataset 1: NHANES 2021-2023 (Primary Training Data)
----------------------------------------------------------

DATA SOURCE:
Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. (2023). National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) 2021-2023. Available from: https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/nhanes/

COLLECTION METHOD:
NHANES is a nationally representative survey of the United States population conducted by the CDC. Data is collected through:
- In-person interviews
- Physical examinations
- Laboratory tests
- 24-hour dietary recall interviews

TOTAL PARTICIPANTS: 2,660 individuals

FILES USED FROM NHANES:
1. DEMO_L.xpt - Demographics (age, gender)
2. BMX_L.xpt - Body Measurements (height, weight, waist circumference)
3. GLU_L.xpt - Glucose (fasting blood glucose)
4. DIQ_L.xpt - Diabetes Questionnaire (family history, diagnosis)
5. DR1TOT_L.xpt - Dietary Day 1 Total (carbs, fiber, fat, protein, sugar, calories)

MERGING PROCESS:
All five files are merged using SEQN (Sequence Number), which is a unique participant identifier.

MERGING CODE (from process_nhanes_2021_2023.py):
```
# Load all files
demo = pd.read_sas('DEMO_L.xpt')
bmx = pd.read_sas('BMX_L.xpt')
glu = pd.read_sas('GLU_L.xpt')
diq = pd.read_sas('DIQ_L.xpt')
diet = pd.read_sas('DR1TOT_L.xpt')

# Merge by SEQN
df = demo.merge(bmx, on='SEQN', how='inner')
df = df.merge(glu, on='SEQN', how='inner')
df = df.merge(diq, on='SEQN', how='inner')
df = df.merge(diet, on='SEQN', how='inner')
```

EXTRACTED FEATURES:
After merging, the following features are extracted:

DEMOGRAPHIC FEATURES:
- age: Age in years (from RIDAGEYR variable)
- gender: Male or Female (from RIAGENDR variable, 1=Male, 2=Female)

BODY MEASUREMENT FEATURES:
- height_cm: Standing height in centimeters (from BMXHT variable)
- weight_kg: Weight in kilograms (from BMXWT variable)
- waist_cm: Waist circumference in centimeters (from BMXWAIST variable)
- BMI: Body Mass Index, calculated as weight_kg / (height_cm/100)²

GLUCOSE FEATURES:
- fasting_glucose: Fasting blood glucose in mg/dL (from LBXGLU variable)

DIABETES FEATURES:
- family_history: Whether parents or siblings have diabetes (from DIQ175A-G variables)
- has_diabetes: Whether participant has been diagnosed with diabetes (from DIQ010 variable)

DIETARY FEATURES:
- carbs_g: Total carbohydrates in grams (from DR1TCARB variable)
- fiber_g: Dietary fiber in grams (from DR1TFIBE variable)
- fat_g: Total fat in grams (from DR1TTFAT variable)
- protein_g: Protein in grams (from DR1TPROT variable)
- sugar_g: Total sugars in grams (from DR1TSUGR variable)
- energy_kcal: Total energy in kilocalories (from DR1TKCAL variable)

DERIVED FEATURES:
- available_carbs_g: Calculated as carbs_g - fiber_g (only digestible carbs)
- risk_level: Classified based on fasting glucose using ADA 2024 thresholds:
  * Low: fasting_glucose < 100 mg/dL
  * Mid: fasting_glucose 100-125 mg/dL
  * High: fasting_glucose >= 126 mg/dL

FINAL OUTPUT:
File: nhanes_2021_2023_processed.csv
Rows: 2,660 participants
Columns: 19 features


3.4.3 Dataset 2: Foster-Powell 2002 (Glycemic Index Values)
------------------------------------------------------------

DATA SOURCE:
Foster-Powell K, Holt SHA, Brand-Miller JC. (2002). International table of glycemic index and glycemic load values: 2002. American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 76(1):5-56.

WHY THIS SOURCE?
Foster-Powell 2002 is the GOLD STANDARD reference for Glycemic Index values. It is:
- Peer-reviewed and published in a top-tier nutrition journal
- Based on controlled human studies with standardized methodology
- Widely cited in diabetes research (over 3,000 citations)
- Recommended by the American Diabetes Association

WHY PDF EXTRACTION?
The Foster-Powell 2002 data exists ONLY in PDF format as tables in the published journal article. There is NO machine-readable database available. Therefore, manual extraction from the PDF was necessary.

EXTRACTION PROCESS:
1. Downloaded PDF from American Journal of Clinical Nutrition
2. Manually transcribed data from Tables 1-6 in the article
3. Verified values by cross-referencing with Atkinson 2021 update
4. Created CSV file: gi_database_research_only.csv

TOTAL FOODS: 200 foods

EXTRACTED FIELDS:
- food_name: Descriptive name of the food (e.g., "White bread", "Apple, raw")
- gi_value: Glycemic Index (0-100 scale, glucose reference)
- gl_value: Glycemic Load per serving
- serving_size_g: Standard serving size in grams
- available_carbs_g: Available carbohydrates per serving (from GI study)
- food_category: Category (Bakery, Fruits, Legumes, etc.)

3.4.5 Integration Method 1: NHANES + Foster-Powell (Training Data)
-------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE:
Combine NHANES health data with GI/GL values to train RF Model 1 (Glucose Predictor)

CHALLENGE:
NHANES provides TOTAL DAILY dietary intake (e.g., "participant consumed 250g carbs today") but does NOT provide individual food items. Foster-Powell provides GI/GL for INDIVIDUAL FOODS but not for total daily intake.

SOLUTION:
Use a CATEGORY-BASED APPROACH to estimate Glycemic Load for each participant.

APPROACH:
Since we cannot match individual foods, we use the AVERAGE GI for a typical American mixed diet.

TYPICAL AMERICAN DIET COMPOSITION (from USDA dietary guidelines):
- 40% Grains/Cereals (average GI = 60)
- 20% Fruits/Vegetables (average GI = 50)
- 15% Dairy (average GI = 35)
- 15% Sugars/Sweets (average GI = 58)
- 10% Legumes/Nuts (average GI = 34)

WEIGHTED AVERAGE GI CALCULATION:
Mixed Diet GI = (0.40 × 60) + (0.20 × 50) + (0.15 × 35) + (0.15 × 58) + (0.10 × 34)
Mixed Diet GI = 24 + 10 + 5.25 + 8.7 + 3.4
Mixed Diet GI = 51.35 ≈ 52.5

GLYCEMIC LOAD FORMULA (Salmerón 1997):
GL = (available_carbs_g × GI) / 100

EXAMPLE CALCULATION:
Participant has:
- Total carbs: 250g
- Fiber: 20g
- Available carbs: 250 - 20 = 230g

Glycemic Load = (230 × 52.5) / 100 = 120.75

INTEGRATION CODE (from merge_gi_with_nhanes.py):
```
# Calculate average GI for typical mixed diet
typical_diet_gi = (0.40 * 60 + 0.20 * 50 + 0.15 * 35 + 0.15 * 58 + 0.10 * 34)

# Calculate GL for each participant
nhanes['glycemic_load'] = (nhanes['available_carbs_g'] * typical_diet_gi) / 100
```

RESULT:
Each NHANES participant now has:
- All original health features (age, BMI, glucose, etc.)
- All original dietary features (carbs, fiber, fat, protein)
- NEW FEATURE: glycemic_load (calculated from GI database)

OUTPUT FILE:
nhanes_with_gl.csv (2,660 participants with GL values)

CITATION FOR GL FORMULA:
Salmerón J, et al. (1997). Dietary fiber, glycemic load, and risk of NIDDM in women. JAMA, 277(6):472-477.


3.4.6 Integration Method 2: Foster-Powell + USDA (Food Database)
-----------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE:
Enrich Foster-Powell GI database with complete nutritional information from USDA for the food lookup database used in the application.

CHALLENGE:
Foster-Powell provides GI/GL values but LACKS complete nutritional information (protein, fat, fiber, calories). USDA provides complete nutrition but LACKS GI/GL values.

SOLUTION:
CROSS-MATCH food names between Foster-Powell and USDA using FUZZY STRING MATCHING.

FUZZY MATCHING ALGORITHM:
Uses Python's SequenceMatcher to calculate similarity scores between food names.

SIMILARITY SCORE CALCULATION:
Similarity = (2 × matching_characters) / (length_string1 + length_string2)

EXAMPLE:
Foster-Powell food: "Apple, raw"
USDA food: "Apples, raw, with skin"
Similarity = 0.85 (85% match)

MATCHING PROCESS (from match_gi_with_usda_local.py):
```
for gi_food in gi_database:
    best_match = None
    best_score = 0
    
    for usda_food in usda_database:
        score = SequenceMatcher(None, gi_food_name, usda_food_name).ratio()
        
        if score > best_score:
            best_score = score
            best_match = usda_food
    
    if best_score >= 0.5:
        # Accept match
        gi_food['usda_match'] = best_match
        gi_food['match_score'] = best_score
```

MATCHING RESULTS:
Total foods: 200
High confidence matches (score >= 0.8): 138 foods (69%)
Low confidence matches (score 0.5-0.8): 62 foods (31%)
No matches (score < 0.5): 0 foods (0%)

MANUAL VERIFICATION:
The 62 low-confidence matches were manually reviewed:
- 27 foods verified as correct matches
- 35 foods flagged for review (still have USDA data, but lower confidence)

FINAL ENRICHED DATABASE:
Each food now has:
- GI value (from Foster-Powell 2002)
- GL value (from Foster-Powell 2002)
- Calories (from USDA)
- Carbohydrates (from USDA)
- Protein (from USDA)
- Fat (from USDA)
- Fiber (from USDA)
- Sodium (from USDA)

EXAMPLE ENRICHED ENTRY:
Food: All-Bran cereal
GI: 42 (Foster-Powell)
GL: 9 (Foster-Powell)
Calories: 333 kcal per 100g (USDA)
Carbs: 46g per 100g (USDA)
Protein: 14g per 100g (USDA)
Fat: 3.5g per 100g (USDA)
Fiber: 27g per 100g (USDA)
Sodium: 660mg per 100g (USDA)

OUTPUT FILE:
gi_database_enriched.csv (200 foods with complete nutrition)

CITATION FOR MATCHING METHOD:
Ratcliff JW, Metzener DE. (1988). Pattern-matching: the gestalt approach. Dr. Dobb's Journal, 13(7):46.


3.4.7 Integration Method 3: USDA Standalone (Food Lookup Database)
-------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE:
Provide comprehensive food search database for users to log meals in the application.

PROCESS:
The complete USDA FoodData Central database (343,877 foods) is processed to create a searchable food lookup database.

PROCESSING STEPS:
1. Load USDA food.csv and food_nutrient.csv
2. Extract relevant nutrients (carbs, protein, fat, fiber, calories, sodium)
3. Calculate diabetes risk classification for each food
4. Save as food_lookup_database.csv

RISK CLASSIFICATION RULES:
LOW RISK: Carbs < 15g AND Sugar < 5g AND Fiber > 5g
HIGH RISK: Carbs > 60g OR Sugar > 20g OR (Carbs > 30g AND Fiber < 3g)
MEDIUM RISK: All other combinations

RISK DISTRIBUTION:
Low Risk: 2,870 foods (0.8%)
Medium Risk: 202,676 foods (58.9%)
High Risk: 138,331 foods (40.2%)

OUTPUT FILE:
food_lookup_database.csv (343,877 foods)

USAGE IN APPLICATION:
When users search for foods in the meal logging interface, the system queries this database and returns matching foods with complete nutritional information and pre-calculated risk classification.


3.4.8 Summary: How All Three Datasets Work Together
----------------------------------------------------

TRAINING PHASE (Machine Learning Model Development):

STEP 1: NHANES + Foster-Powell → Training Data
- NHANES provides health metrics and dietary intake
- Foster-Powell provides GI values to calculate Glycemic Load
- Combined dataset trains RF Model 1 (Glucose Predictor) and RF Model 2 (Risk Classifier)

STEP 2: Foster-Powell + USDA → Enriched GI Database
- Foster-Powell provides GI/GL values
- USDA provides complete nutritional information
- Combined database validates that RF Model 1 features (protein, fat, fiber) are accurate


DEPLOYMENT PHASE (User-Facing Application):

STEP 3: USDA Standalone → Food Lookup Database
- Users search for foods by name
- System returns nutritional information from USDA
- System calculates meal totals (calories, carbs, protein, fat, fiber)

STEP 4: Meal Data + RF Models → Predictions
- User's meal data (carbs, fat, protein, fiber) extracted from USDA
- User's health data (age, BMI, gender, fasting glucose) from profile
- RF Model 1 predicts 1-hour post-meal glucose
- RF Model 2 classifies diabetes risk


VISUAL FLOW DIAGRAM:

TRAINING:
[NHANES 2021-2023] + [Foster-Powell GI] → [Training Data] → [RF Models]
        ↓                      ↓
   Health Data          GI/GL Values
   Dietary Data

VALIDATION:
[Foster-Powell GI] + [USDA Nutrition] → [Enriched GI Database] → [Verify Features]
        ↓                    ↓
   GI/GL Values      Complete Nutrition

DEPLOYMENT:
[USDA Food Database] → [User Searches Food] → [Extract Nutrients] → [RF Models] → [Predictions]
        ↓
   343,877 Foods


3.4.9 Rationale for Three-Dataset Approach
-------------------------------------------

QUESTION: Why not use a single dataset?

ANSWER: No single dataset contains all required information.

NHANES ALONE:
- Has health data and dietary intake
- LACKS GI/GL values
- LACKS individual food nutritional details
- Cannot train glucose prediction model without GI/GL

FOSTER-POWELL ALONE:
- Has GI/GL values
- LACKS health data (age, BMI, glucose)
- LACKS complete nutritional information
- Only 200 foods (too small for food database)
- Cannot train models without health data

USDA ALONE:
- Has complete nutritional information
- LACKS GI/GL values
- LACKS health data
- Cannot calculate glycemic load
- Cannot train diabetes risk model

COMBINED APPROACH:
- NHANES provides health data for model training
- Foster-Powell provides GI/GL for glycemic load calculation
- USDA provides complete nutrition for food database
- All three together enable comprehensive system


3.4.10 Limitations and Considerations
--------------------------------------

LIMITATION 1: Category-Based GI Estimation
NHANES does not provide individual food items, so we use average GI for typical mixed diet. This is less accurate than individual food matching but is the best approach given data constraints.

LIMITATION 2: Fuzzy Matching Uncertainty
35 foods (17.5%) have low-confidence matches between Foster-Powell and USDA. These foods still have USDA nutritional data but may not be exact matches.

LIMITATION 3: US Population Only
NHANES represents US population. Dietary patterns and food availability differ in other countries, limiting generalizability.

LIMITATION 4: Simulated Glucose Target
NHANES does not include post-meal glucose measurements, so RF Model 1 target variable is simulated using clinical formula. Real-world validation with CGM data is needed.


3.4.11 Data Quality Assurance
------------------------------

QUALITY CHECK 1: Missing Data
- NHANES: Removed participants with missing dietary data (566 participants excluded)
- Foster-Powell: All 200 foods have complete GI/GL values
- USDA: Removed foods with incomplete nutritional data

QUALITY CHECK 2: Outlier Detection
- Fasting glucose: Capped at 300 mg/dL (physiological maximum)
- BMI: Removed values < 15 or > 50 (likely data entry errors)
- Dietary intake: Removed extreme values (> 5000 kcal/day)

QUALITY CHECK 3: Consistency Validation
- Verified that available_carbs = total_carbs - fiber
- Verified that risk_level matches ADA 2024 thresholds
- Verified that GL correlates strongly with carbohydrate intake (r > 0.95)
